Network Topology for Disciplinary Research

A Brief Introduction for Applying Social Network Analysis on Intellectual Cohesion

Introduction

My MA study in Taiwan focused on Social Network Analysis (SNA): a way to analyze and visualize ties between individuals in communities. I adopted it as the primary tactic in my longitudinal research on Digital Humanities, a relatively new discipline. In the research, I focus more on how the discipline adopts methodology and knowledge from existing disciplines, also on how researchers collaborate. In this page, I'll brief introduce four models in SNA from the method I adopted to recognize collaborative patterns with network topology.

How to read the networks

When using SNA to analyze inter-disciplinary research, we usually use authors, Articles and Journals as the nodes, and the co-author, co-citation, co-cited as the links. Co-author network (How many times the authors working together on articles) can visualize the collaborative patterns; Co-citation and the co-cited networks (How many references the articles sharing, and how many times the articles are cited by the same article) can show the flow of the knowledge.

Plural-worlds

In this kind of social network, the nodes are clustered in different groups with no link between the groups. This means that there are no knowledge sharing or collaboration between these groups.

Usually, we can find this kind of networks on the disciplines that differ from each other on their theories and methodologies.

Small-world

In this kind of social network, the nodes are clustered in different groups but have little links between the groups. This means that there are little knowledge sharing or collaboration between these groups.

Usually, we can find this kind of networks on the early stage of an inter-disciplinary collaboration, researchers from different backgrounds started to use the knowledge from other disciplines or collaborate on researchs.

Structural cohesion

As you can see, the links between different groups are dense. This means that there are lots of knowledge exchange and collaboration between these groups.

When we see this kind of inter-disciplinary networks, it usually means that researchers from different backgrounds are sharing knowledge or collaborate with each other frequently. There are chances that there will be a new discipline borned between these existing disciplines.

Star production model

In this kind of social network, There are a small group of nodes in the center and linked with each other tight. all other nodes are linked to ther center group but not linked with nodes in the periphery.

This kind of network means that this discipline has a solid sore, some of the authors, journals or researches are having high authourity. This kind of discipline, just opposite to the structural cohesion network, tend to have clear border.
