Hello, I'm Yun-Jen Cheng.

I'm a UX designer from Taiwan now living in Ann Arbor, MI.
I focus on research-based design and aim for fulfilling the needs of the users.

Briefly About Me

My career path up until now has been hugely shaped by changes in the field of information science, especially the rise of crowdsourced data collection projects. After finishing my master's degree in library and information science, I co-founded Lightbox Photo Library, a Taiwan-based nonprofit organization focused on crowdsourcing photography information. Through my work, I was exposed to the many challenges of working in the intersecting fields of front-end developing, social media, and crowdsourced projects.

My Academic Experiences

I earned my BA and MA degrees from the Department of Library and Information Science at National Taiwan University. During my MA study, I combined my interests on communities, collaborations, and data visualization, adopted Social Network Analysis as the primary tactic in my research on Digital Humanities.

Founding an Non-profit

After graduation, I joined a startup team to launch Lightbox Photo Library, the first crowdsourced photographic library in Taiwan. In this team, I got the chance to combine my interest in visual media and interdisciplinary collaboration with my skills in front-end development and information science. Across three years of experience implementing crowdsourcing projects and multidisciplinary collaborations.

What I Focus Now

With my experience as a front-end developer for a contemporary art institute, my visual media skills, and my interest in information experience design motivated me to deepen my understanding of UX design.
