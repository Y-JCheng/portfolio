Umove for newbies


Property rental platform designed for the international students in Umich to find a suitable place to live.

Finding a place to live is the very first problem international students are facing, which can highly impact their future development.

Studies shows that the satisfaction of housing may influence students’ academic and social development, especially in the first two years. However, most of international students are having obstacles looking for a place to live.

This project address the inability of international students to seek for a suitable place to rent. I explore the obstacles these students are facing, and find ways to tackle these challenges with design solutions.

Research - Interview

Interview into Why international students are not finding the most suitable place

In my research, I drew the attention on Ann Arbor, a classic college town which ranked 4 for the popularity of the international students in the states.

I meet with 4 interviewees and ask them about their experience looking for a place to live in Ann Arbor. During the interview, they are asked to talk about the following:

  1. Their former living experiences.
  2. How they find the place currently live in?
  3. Their satisfaction with the place they live in.
  4. The most important critirias of looking for a place.
  5. What makes them unsatisfied with the current place?

According to the interviews, Most of them are not satisfied with the current place and looking for opportunities to move.

Also, I conclude these four pain points

Expose to too much Information

There are lots of properties for rent in Ann Arbor, but those information are scatter in different websites. Also, while most of them are not fitting the critirias, international students feel frustrated filter through them one by one.

Lack of time for making best decision

Most international students have less than 5 months looking for the room, it may seemed easy, but they have to deal with other problems (including apply for visa, prepare for tuition, etc.) at the same time. Also, these students are mostly doing their jobs or finishing their degrees during the time searching for the rooms.

Can't see the room in other countries

Most of the properties do not have enough image for the students to check the environment inside and outside the property. But they are not able to do a room-viewing without being there.

Have problems communicate with the landlords

Some of the students are not confident with their English ability, hence they tend to avoid verbal communication with the landlords. This makes it harder for them to get better understanding of the properties.

Research - Competitor Analysis

Based on my interviews and some research, I collect four main ways people looking for housing informations.

UM Housing site

UM’s official housing site provides housing information about both on-campus and off-campus. As one of the main channel the students looking for housing information, it includes structured information, but the amount of information it provide is overwhelmingly huge.

MTSA life database

A wiki-like forum constructed by Michigan Taiwanese Student Association (MTSA). The housing contents are maintained by a few students. Provide few housing experiences. However, the information is outdated and very few on the amounts.

Facebook groups

Students of UM formed Facebook groups as a platform to share housing information. Communications through Facebook are more convenient than E-mail, but the information in the groups are messy and hard to search with.

Apartment List

A web-based apartment pairing system. Users provide preferences and the system provides recommandations. However, their are too much questions to answer during the process, and only the apartments can be found.

I compare four competitors and find out what should be addressed in my design.

More is not always better

There are many options for the students, but with limited time, it's important to keep the results precise.

The devil is in the detail

It's important to see through every corner of the rooms. But there's not much solutions for this.

Language matters

It's already very convenient to contact anyone online. But having English as second language really is a barrier of communication.

Persona

Shiao-Ming Wang
Taiwan
22 years old
undergraduate

  • Motivation:
    • He’s going to move to Ann Arbor in months
    • He’s looking forward for new life in the States
    • He’s an out-going person who love to know more people
  • Goals:
    • To find a place to live with reasonable costs
    • To learn more details about the Ann Arbor and the campus
  • Frustrations:
    • The weather is so differed between his hometown and Ann Arbor
    • He has limited experience communicating in English
    • He hasn’t had any experience lives in a relatively small city
    • He doesn’t have any experience renting a house/apartment
